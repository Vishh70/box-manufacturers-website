Understanding Corrugated Board Structure

Corrugated board is made up of a fluted (wavy) layer sandwiched between flat paper liners. The number of layers determines the "ply" count:

  • 3 Ply (Single Wall): One fluted layer between two liners. Ideal for lightweight items, e-commerce shipping, and retail packaging. Bursting strength: 8â€“12 kg/cmÂ².
  • 5 Ply (Double Wall): Two fluted layers with three liners. Perfect for heavier items, electronics, and fragile products. Bursting strength: 14â€“20 kg/cmÂ².
  • 7 Ply (Triple Wall): Three fluted layers with four liners. Used for industrial-grade packaging, machinery, and export shipments. Bursting strength: 20â€“30 kg/cmÂ².

Choosing the Right Flute Profile

The flute (the wavy middle layer) comes in different sizes, each offering different benefits:

  • A Flute (5mm): Excellent cushioning. Best for fragile products and stacking.
  • B Flute (3mm): Good crush resistance. Ideal for die-cut boxes, shelf displays, and e-commerce mailers.
  • C Flute (4mm): Most common. Good balance of cushioning and stacking strength. Used for shipping boxes.
  • E Flute (1.5mm): Thin profile with excellent printing surface. Perfect for retail packaging and printed boxes.
  • BC Flute (double wall): Combines B and C for maximum protection. Used in 5-ply heavy-duty boxes.

Determining the Right Size

The ideal box size should be only slightly larger than the product it holds (2â€“3 cm clearance on each side for cushioning material). Oversized boxes lead to:

  • Higher dimensional weight shipping charges
  • Products shifting and getting damaged
  • More void fill material needed
  • Higher material costs
